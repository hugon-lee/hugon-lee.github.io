Hi, I am Hugon Lee, a Ph.D. candidate in Mechanical Engineering at the Korea Advanced Institute of Science and Technology (KAIST), advised by Prof. Seunghwa Ryu. I recently defended my Ph.D. dissertation and will officially receive my degree in August 2026. After graduation, I will be joining postdoctoral fellowship at InnoCORE PRISM-AI center at KAIST.

My research focuses on the micromechanical analysis of composite materials, particularly interface and interphase effects, using computational, analytical, and data-driven methods. I have also studied process and property modeling of materials to describe experimental outcomes, incorporating machine learning for design and optimization in data-scarce scenarios. My recent research interests include applying physics-informed machine learning and agentic AI frameworks to the analysis of composites.
